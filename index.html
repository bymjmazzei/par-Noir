How It Works

Your digital identity, your rules. Every time.

Security Architecture

Layer 1: Triple-Component Authentication Your pN requires three components to decrypt: the pN file, your pN name, and your passcode. Without all three, your data remains completely inaccessible.
Layer 2: Encrypted IPFS Storage Your credentials derive an encryption key that secures your metadata on IPFS. The decentralized storage ensures no central point of failure while maintaining complete privacy.
Layer 3: Zero-Knowledge Proofs Cryptographic proofs generated from your encrypted metadata allow verification of facts without revealing underlying data. Prove you're over 18 without sharing your age or birthday.
Layer 4: Permission-Based Access Control Every data access request requires your explicit approval. You control what's shared, when it's shared, and with whom—no automatic data sharing ever occurs.

Real-World Example

Scenario: You want to access an age-restricted platform

  1. You create your pN and attest your birthday to your identity
  2. You use your pN to access the platform
  3. The platform requests: "Prove you're over 18"
  4. You choose to share only the fact that you're over 18—without revealing your age or birthday
  5. Access granted, privacy maintained
